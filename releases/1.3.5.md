## What's New

- **Channels are now identified by country, so the same channel name in two countries no longer collides.** As we add countries beyond the US, names stop being unique — the US's **ABC** and Australia's **ABC** are different broadcasters with different logos, different schedules and different feeds, but to the software they used to look like one channel, so whichever was loaded last quietly overwrote the other. Every channel now carries its country: the playlist and guide identify it as `us-abc` rather than `abc`, and its logo comes from that country's artwork.
  - **Your player will re-detect its channels once, on the upgrade.** The channel ID a playlist advertises is what Jellyfin, Plex and Emby store as a channel's identity, so changing it means your player sees a fresh lineup. **Expect to re-pick your favourites, and to re-point any recording rules or series passes** at the new channels. There is no way around this — it is the same one-time cost as renaming a channel — and it is what buys the guide staying attached to the right broadcaster from here on.
  - Playlist and guide URLs are unchanged, so **no reconfiguration is needed** — a refresh in your player picks everything up. Channels you had hidden, and any custom channel numbers you set, are carried across automatically.
- **Choose which countries you want channels for.** **Settings → Channel Countries** lists every country available, all enabled by default. Turn one off and its channels leave the playlist, the guide, the dashboard and the search — everywhere, not just the channel list. New countries arrive switched **on** at the bottom of the list, so you are never silently opted out of something new; drag the list to set the order your country tabs appear in.
- **A new channel layout, and you pick which one you use.** The channel list can now be browsed as **country tabs with channel cards** — a market filter scoped to whichever country tab you are on, a search tab that spans every country at once, and a Clear button, since a long market list was tedious to reset by hand. Channels from your own custom sources don't belong to any country, so they get a **Custom** tab of their own at the end of the row, and each card names the source it came from. The classic list is unchanged and still there: **Settings → Layout** switches between them at any time, and your choice is remembered. Filters live in the URL, so a refresh — or a bookmark, or a link you send yourself — lands exactly where you were, instead of snapping back to a filter you set days ago.
- **Sports options moved out of the environment and into the dashboard.** Whether to carry sports events at all, whether to run **sports-only** (events, no channels), and **which leagues to carry**, are now settings in **Settings → Sports** — they used to be `SPORTS_EVENTS`, `SPORTS_MODE` and `LEAGUES` environment variables, which meant a container restart to change your mind. They now take effect on the next refresh, and the league list stays current as new leagues become available. Each league in the picker is shown with its badge, so you can pick from the list at a glance.
- **The dashboard's Jellyfin and Plex setup steps are up to date again.** The setup wizard and **Settings → Connect Jellyfin or Plex** were still telling Jellyfin users to turn *on* the tuner's **“Auto-loop live streams”** option — the setting 1.3.4 asked you to turn **off** (with it on, Jellyfin re-encodes every channel and the picture freezes). Both now say to leave it off, explain why, and list the rest of the recommended tuner settings. The Plex steps now point at the **“Have an M3U-based tuner”** prompt for the playlist and the XMLTV prompt for the guide (they previously sent you to the guide prompt for both), and note that Plex requires an active Plex Pass. The wizard's final step now covers Plex as well as Jellyfin.
- **Live streams are more reliable when a feed goes bad.** Three fixes to how the picture and sound hold up: when a channel falls back to a backup feed the **sound no longer drops out** — the audio stays in step with the video, and the backup is chosen to be one your player can still *decode* (a feed that records its audio differently used to leave you a perfect picture and silence); a feed carrying **no sound or no picture is now detected and skipped** rather than going to air broken; and a **momentary gap in a feed is retried** instead of abandoned instantly, so a lossy source stops looking like the stream keeps dropping — and when a piece really is lost for good, the stream closes over it so you skip a few seconds rather than freeze on a hole. (Feeds in a format we can't inspect are left alone, so nothing that works today stops working.)
- **The match detail page now tells you what happened, and keeps up.** It opens with a **timeline** — goals, cards, who scored and when, each on the side of the team that caused it — for any sport we carry. Live **statistics** (possession, shots, corners, fouls) now refresh in step with the score instead of roughly once an hour (and are there before kick-off, not halfway through); a **tournament** fixture shows the two sides' previous meetings (that panel used to be blank when there was no season series to show); and a **finished game stays on the Sports page**, marked **FINAL** with the score, until the day is over where you are — instead of vanishing the moment the final whistle went. It still leaves your playlist and guide as soon as it ends.

## Update

Already running Rebel IPTV? Update to this version — your data (the `iptv-data` volume) is kept. From the dashboard, click **Update now** on the Dashboard or in **Settings → Server** (on the `:latest` image with the Docker socket mounted). Or from the command line:

```bash
docker pull rebeliptv/iptv:1.3.5
docker compose up -d
```

Setting up a **new** instance? The step-by-step guide at **https://www.rebeliptv.com** builds a Docker configuration for your system — ports, timezone, in-app updates, and more — so you copy a setup that fits your box instead of a generic one.

Image: `rebeliptv/iptv:1.3.5` (also published as `rebeliptv/iptv:latest`).

| URL | Description |
|-----|-------------|
| `http://<server>:8080/playlist?key=YOUR_KEY` | M3U playlist |

| `http://<server>:8080/epg.gz?key=YOUR_KEY` | EPG guide (gzip) |
